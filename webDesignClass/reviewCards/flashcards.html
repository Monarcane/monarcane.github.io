Filmmaking Terms


C-Stand
A robust, metal stand commonly used in film and photography to support various equipment, especially for positioning lights, light modifiers like flags and silks, and backdrops.
Tripod
A three-legged stand for supporting a camera or other apparatus.
Gimble
A device that stabilizes a camera, especially during handheld shooting, by counteracting movement and vibrations to produce smooth, steady footage.
Deadcat
A synthetic fur cover for microphones that blocks wind from hitting the microphone, reducing wind noise in the recording.
A-Roll
This is the core footage, including the main action, dialogue, and key plot points.
B-Roll
Supplementary footage that is used to add visual interest, context, and flow to the story.
Pre-Production
The phase before actual filming begins, including finalizing the script, hiring the crew, finding locations, and creating schedules.
Post-Production
The phase of filmmaking and video production that occurs after filming is complete. It involves editing, adding sound, visual effects, and other enhancements to create the final, polished product.
Call Sheet
A document used in film and television production that provides all the essential details about a day's shooting schedule, including location, cast call times, and contact information for the crew.
